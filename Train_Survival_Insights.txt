Train Passenger Survival Analysis Insights

1) Gender-Based Survival Insight
Female passengers had a significantly higher survival rate than male passengers. This suggests that women were given higher priority during the evacuation process.

2) Passenger Class Impact
Passengers belonging to first and second class had better survival chances compared to those in third class. This shows that socio-economic status played a strong role in survival outcomes.

3) Age-Based Survival Pattern
The average age of the passengers is around 29.7 years. Younger passengers, especially children, show a slightly higher chance of survival compared to older passengers.

4) Overall Survival Count
Survival numbers are much lower compared to the total number of passengers. This indicates that the accident had a high fatality rate overall.

Conclusion:
Survival was influenced by gender, passenger class, and age. Females and higher-class passengers had the highest survival rate.
